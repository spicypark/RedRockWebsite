Who We Are

Built in Utah. Competing Everywhere.

Red Rock Robotics fields two FIRST Robotics Competition teams — Team 3006 and Team 2726 — from Salt Lake City, Utah. We believe robotics should be accessible to every student who wants to learn, build, and compete. Our teams design and build robots from scratch each season, competing against thousands of teams worldwide.

Our Organization

Who We Are

Red Rock Robotics is a FIRST Robotics Competition program based in Salt Lake City, Utah. We operate two competitive FRC teams — Team 3006 and Team 2726 — composed of over 50 active students from across the Salt Lake Valley. Both teams compete under the same program umbrella, sharing resources, mentorship, and a commitment to excellence.

Founded on the belief that hands-on engineering experience should be available to all students regardless of background or prior experience, Red Rock Robotics has grown into one of Utah's most active robotics programs. We are supported by a dedicated group of more than 10 mentors and coaches who bring real-world industry expertise to our students every build season.

Team 3006

Team 3006 is our flagship team, with a strong competition history across regional and championship events. Team 3006 has consistently pushed the boundaries of robot design, field strategy, and drive performance. Students on this team take on demanding technical challenges each season, from mechanical design to advanced autonomous programming.

Team 2726

Team 2726 was established to expand our program's reach and give more students the opportunity to compete at the FRC level. This team operates with the same high standards as Team 3006 — every student is a valued contributor to robot design, software, strategy, and outreach. Team 2726 has quickly grown into a competitive force in its own right.

Our Subteams

Both teams are organized into specialized subteams: Hardware, covering mechanical design, fabrication, and assembly; Software, responsible for robot programming and autonomous routines; Strategy, handling scouting, match planning, and drive coaching; HPR (Human & Public Relations), our business subteam managing outreach, sponsorships, branding, and communications; and dedicated Electrical and Safety roles ensuring our robots are built and operated reliably.

The FIRST Acronym

FIRST stands for For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology. Founded in 1989 by inventor Dean Kamen, FIRST is a global nonprofit that builds STEM skills in young people alongside the confidence, knowledge, and life skills to succeed. Today, FIRST reaches over 680,000 youth annually across more than 110 countries.

FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC)

The FIRST Robotics Competition is the premier high school robotics competition in the world. Each January, FIRST reveals a brand-new game challenge. Teams of students, working alongside professional mentors, have just six weeks to design, build, program, and test a robot capable of competing in that year's game. Robots then compete at regional events, and the best teams advance to the annual FIRST Championship.

FRC is often described as "the hardest fun you'll ever have." Beyond technical skills — engineering, programming, electronics, and fabrication — students develop leadership, communication, time management, and teamwork throughout every season.

Gracious Professionalism®

At the heart of FIRST is a concept called Gracious Professionalism — the idea that you can compete fiercely while treating every person with respect and kindness. FIRST teams are famous for helping other teams, even direct competitors, because the community values learning and sportsmanship. Red Rock Robotics carries this philosophy into everything we do.
